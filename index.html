MiG-21PD & MiG-25BP

MiG-21PD

The MiG-21PD was an experimental Soviet aircraft developed in the 1960s. It was based on the MiG-21 and featured additional lift engines to improve short takeoff performance.

MiG-21PD Specifications

  • Country: Soviet Union
  • Role: Experimental interceptor
  • Engines: 1 main engine + 2 lift engines
  • First flight: 1966
  • Status: Prototype only

MiG-25BP

The MiG-25BP (Foxbat) was a high-speed Soviet interceptor designed to counter high-altitude threats. It prioritized speed and altitude over maneuverability.

MiG-25BP Specifications

  • Country: Soviet Union
  • Role: Interceptor
  • Engines: 2× Tumansky R-15 turbojets
  • Top speed: Mach 2.8+
  • Service entry: 1970
